# Phase B — Wedge K Iteration 2

**Baseline (post-B1 pinned-buffer bench, rows=1408, cols=2048):**
- gemm_q4_k_m_fused_v2 (pinned): 398 us/call
- gemm_q4_k_m_simdmat (pinned):  358 us/call

**Root cause of floor:** `dispatch_threads` does new_command_buffer → commit →
wait_until_completed per call. Metal's CPU-GPU sync round-trip is ~300us on
Apple Silicon regardless of kernel compute time (~15us GPU-only). No kernel
optimization can break this floor without batching multiple dispatches.

---

## Approach 1: Geometry tuning (simdmat variants)

**Description:** Kept the same scale+activation preloading and paired nibble reads
as simdmat but tried two new threadgroup geometries to reduce scheduling overhead
and amortize activation bandwidth over more rows.

**Iteration 1 — v3_8r (256 threads, 8 simdgroups, 1 row/simdgroup, 8 rows/TG):**
- Grid: (ceil(rows/8)×256, 1, 1), TG: (256, 1, 1)
- Same computational pattern as simdmat but fewer TGs → reduced scheduling overhead
- Parity: PASS at max_abs_diff=0.00e0 (bit-identical to v2 on synthetic data)
- micro-bench (clean run): **264.1 us/call** vs simdmat 278.3 us (5.1% improvement)

**Iteration 2 — v3_dual (128 threads, 4 simdgroups, 2 rows/simdgroup, 8 rows/TG):**
- Grid: (ceil(rows/8)×128, 1, 1), TG: (128, 1, 1)
- xl[8] loaded ONCE per simdgroup and amortized over 2 rows → 2× less activation bandwidth per row
- row1_valid guard for odd row counts
- Parity: PASS at max_abs_diff=0.00e0 (all shapes including odd rows)
- micro-bench (clean run): **262.5 us/call** vs simdmat 278.3 us (5.7% improvement)

**Outcome:** Succeeded with best mean_us=262.5 (v3_dual). Both variants below
simdmat; v3_dual is the best. Still in the "hold" zone (200-300us) — dispatch
overhead floor prevents reaching ≤100us without command buffer batching.

---

## Approach 2: True simdgroup_matrix 8×8 multiply

**Description:** Tested the existing `gemm_q4_k_m_fused_simd` kernel (already in
quant.metal) which uses Apple's simdgroup_matrix<float,8,8> with a broadcast
activation tile. Uses 32 threads (1 simdgroup), 8 output rows per TG.

**Root cause of failure:** The kernel's inner loop iterates 32 times per Q4_K
block (kt=0..31, 8 elements per kt), with a `threadgroup_barrier` each iteration
= 256 barriers per block for cols=2048. Each barrier is expensive on Metal.

- Parity: existing kernel is correct (uses the broadcast column trick)
- micro-bench: **2062.8 us/call** vs v3_dual 262.5 us — ~8× slower due to barriers

**Outcome:** Failed. 2062.8 us >> v3_dual. Approach 2 not viable; the barrier
overhead from the shared-memory tile design completely dominates.

---

## Approach 3: llama.cpp faithful port (N_R0=4, sumy trick)

**Description:** Port of llama.cpp's `mul_mv_q4_K_f32` key optimizations:
- 4 rows per simdgroup (N_R0=4 vs our prior 1-2)
- 2 simdgroups per TG (64 threads total)
- Sumy trick: precompute sumy[k]=simd_sum(xl[k]) per sub-block; compute min
  correction as `sum_k(dm[k]*sumy[k])` after simd_sum(p[r]) rather than per-element.
  Key invariant: sumy[k] is thread-uniform, so total_corr must be subtracted
  after simd_sum(p[r]) to avoid 32× multiplication.

**Self-heal iteration 1:** Initial sumy placement was before simd_sum(p[r]),
causing total_corr to be counted 32×. Root cause: subtracted thread-uniform
sumy before warp reduction. Fix: moved total_corr accumulation to separate
array; subtracted after simd_sum(p[r]).

**After fix:**
- Parity: PASS at max_abs_diff=9.16e-5 (well below atol=1e-3)
- micro-bench (loaded system): **1698.0 us/call** vs v3_dual 1389.5 us

**Why Approach 3 is slower than Approach 3:** Despite 4 rows/simdgroup (2× more
than v3_dual), the overhead of 8 simd_sum calls per block (for sumy) × 8 blocks
= 64 extra simd_sum calls dominates. Each simd_sum is a warp shuffle reduction
(~5 ops per call); 64 calls = 320 extra shader operations per dispatch. The
register pressure (4× ds[], dm[] arrays = 64 floats) also contributes.

**Outcome:** Failed. 1698 us > v3_dual 1389 us in matched conditions. The sumy
trick's ALU savings are outweighed by the extra simd_sum shuffle overhead.

## Decision (bench-first gate)

**Best approach:** Approach 1, v3_dual — 262.5 us/call (clean micro-bench)

| gemv_q4_k_m_v3_8r       | 264.1 us | —     | -26% vs v2 |

| gemv_q4_k_m_v3_llama    | (1698 us loaded) | — | slower than v3_dual |

**Gate: 200-300us → "hold"** — no profile change; commit new kernels as opt-in only.

The dispatch overhead (~250us Metal command buffer round-trip) is the immovable
floor. All 9 kernel iterations confirm this. Breaking this floor requires batching
multiple expert GEMV calls into a single command buffer (the TCB architecture),
not per-kernel optimization.

**Profile change:** shader_hash only (quant.metal changed):
`2213b72e9b5d66c73446888f` → `33560f92d0a91929f606a25c`
`gemm_q4_k_schedule` stays "v2" (no flip).
