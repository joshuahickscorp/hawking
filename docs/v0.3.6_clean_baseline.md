## Open issues blocking next-wedge design

1. **simd path 0-token regression.** `gemm_q4_k_schedule = "simdgroup"`
   produces `completion_tokens: null` in the bench output despite
   running 8,414 dispatch_batch calls and 102M μs of dispatch wall.
   Either (a) the simdgroup variant produces unprintable / untokenizable
   output that the bench tool can't decode, or (b) the bench tool's
   output-decode path has a bug specific to the simdgroup schedule.
   Parity tests passed at the kernel layer so this is a downstream
   compose issue, not gemm_q4_k_m_fused_simd itself.
2. **Bench harness `completion_tokens` parsing.** The v0.3.6 harness's
   `completion_tokens < 50` discard rule fires for *every* trial because
   the bench tool reports `completion_tokens: null` even on the working
   scalar path. The harness's six-trial protocol cannot complete until
   this is fixed.
3. **gemv_f16 outlier at 220ms mean per call.** 4 calls (the LM head
   at vocab × hidden = ~100k × 2048) take 882ms total = 4% of dispatch
   wall. p99 is 872ms — one call alone is taking nearly a second. A
   proper simdgroup GEMV at this shape should be ~5ms. Tractable wedge
   for a 3-4% perf win.

## Path to flagship

Current: 2.169 dec_tps. Reference llama.cpp Metal on M3 Pro (b9000):
59.6 dec_tps. Gap: **27.5×**.

Structural CB coalescing is largely drained — the v0.3.x series got
+16.5% from the entire campaign. Closing the rest of the gap requires
real kernel-level work, in approximate order of impact:

- **Q4_K_M GEMV rewrite with proper register tiling** (29.5% of wall):
  the v0.3.0 simdgroup_matrix port was naive and lost. A proper version
  with multi-row threadgroups, register tiling, and reduced barrier
  count is the largest single perf wedge available.
- **Multi-expert fused MoE GEMV**: instead of looping 8 experts × 3
  GEMVs per layer, dispatch one kernel that processes all activated
  experts via z-dim (route_id). Reduces dispatch count and improves
  arithmetic intensity per launch. Note: v0.2.0 attempted this as
  "two-stage MoE" and it regressed −79%; the regression is a kernel
  layout issue, not the structural idea — needs a clean re-attempt.
- **MLA flash attention**: replace mla_decode_kernel (5% of wall, 532μs
  mean) with a proper fused softmax+matmul flash variant.
- **MPSGraph integration**: Apple's graph compiler may give 2-5× on
  large GEMV/GEMM. Speculative; requires plumbing a non-Metal-direct
  path.
- **gemv_f16 LM head simdgroup port**: small (4% of wall) but tractable.
