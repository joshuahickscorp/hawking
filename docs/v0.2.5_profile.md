## Findings (clean data)

### Top bottleneck kernel: `gemm_q4_k_m_fused`

- 27.7–38.6% of dispatch wall in steady state (post-JIT-warmup).
- 14,280 calls per 64-token decode = 223 calls/token.
- Mean: 517–553 µs. p50: 384–410 µs. p99: 2,224 µs.
- **This is the dense-layer weight-matrix GEMM** (q4_k quantization, fused). Present in all
  15 dense attention layers (query, key, value, output projections). Absent from MoE layers
  (which use `dispatch_batch` for their GEMM).

### Dense > MoE: the cost inversion

Post-JIT steady state (from baseline run, where no warmup spike):
- `gemm_q4_k_m_fused` (dense) + `gemv_f32_attn` (dense) = 49.9% of dispatch wall.
- `dispatch_batch` (MoE) = 33.9% of dispatch wall.
- Dense layers dominate even though only 15/27 of layers are dense.

### Two-stage MoE regression source

Confirmed: `dispatch_batch` per-call overhead (7.9 ms → 20.8 ms = 2.63× regression).
Root cause is two-stage MoE scheduling, not a data kernel. Two-stage is shelved.

### Dispatch overhead share

`dispatch_batch` is both a compute kernel (MoE execution) and a dispatch overhead proxy.
In baseline (33.9%), most of this is MoE compute, not overhead. Overhead-only (command
buffer encode + submit) is likely <5% based on v0.2.3 estimates.

## v0.3 design implications

**Primary target: `gemm_q4_k_m_fused` simdgroup rewrite**
- Best case: 2–4× speedup on this kernel = 27.7–38.6% of dispatch wall, or ~10–15%
  end-to-end throughput gain.
- This is the dense-layer q4_k GEMM. Rewrite with `simdgroup_matrix` to use matrix-multiply
  hardware units instead of the current scalar/vector approach.
- Target file: `crates/dismantle-core/shaders/` (find `gemm_q4_k_m_fused` kernel).

**Secondary target: `gemv_f32_attn` flash-attention or batched-KV fusion**
- 11–17% of dispatch wall. Mean 421–857 µs (high variance likely JIT warmup + scheduling jitter).
- Flash-attention rewrite or KV-fused attention could cut this 2×.
- Lower priority than gemm rewrite; also more complex.

**Deprioritize: dispatch overhead, two-stage MoE, decode-arena**
- Dispatch overhead is small (<5% without MoE compute).
- Two-stage MoE has 2.63× per-batch regression — not a viable optimization path.
- decode-arena (gpu_buffer_reuse) is already in v0.2.2-metal-safe and not a bottleneck.

**Recommended v0.3 wedge sequence:**
1. `gemm_q4_k_m_fused` simdgroup_matrix port — highest ROI, mechanical kernel rewrite.
2. `gemv_f32_attn` batched-KV or flash-attention — second wedge after gemm validated.
3. Profile again after (1) to re-rank remaining kernels.
