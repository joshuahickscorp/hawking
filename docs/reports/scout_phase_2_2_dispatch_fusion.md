# Phase 2.2 scout — dispatch reduction / kernel fusion (scout, 2026-06-01)

> READ-ONLY scope. Verified against the 0.2 trace (`reports/traces/qwen3b_decode_gpu_prod_64edfbd.json`) and live code on `paradigm/exec`. **Blunt headline up front: this step is almost entirely DEAD-on-arrival against a ship gate.** The trivial ops are 324/616 dispatches but only ~9% of GPU time, and the kill-ledger has already proven CPU encode is 0.51% of wall and `gpu_us` is accurate (host_wall/Σgpu=1.03×) — so the dispatch *count* is not on the critical path. The only fusions that touch real GPU-side time (DRAM round-trips) are tiny. See the kill cross-check; it is load-bearing.

## What is ALREADY fused (do not re-scope — these are the trace lines, not opportunities)
- **`add_rmsnorm_fused`** (`common.metal:144`, 72/tok = 2/layer, 4.98% GPU): the residual-add is *already* folded into BOTH the post-attn norm (`qwen_dense.rs:4238`) and the tail/next-attn norm (`:4543`). The input-layernorm is **hoisted** — there is no separate attn-norm dispatch at layer start (`qwen_dense.rs:3976` comment; the tail fusion at `:4543` produces the next layer's `x_norm_buf`). The final-norm is also folded into the last layer's tail (`:4496-4504`) — no separate final-norm dispatch. **This is the dead Session-F sketch already shipped** (`common.metal:120`).
- **FFN gate+up** fused to ONE `gemm_q4_k_v4_predec_pair` dispatch (`qwen_dense.rs:4275/4296`, the 45.4% line).
- **k+v proj** fused to one `_pair` dispatch (`:4028/4049`).
- **add_rmsnorm_fused_q8** (`common.metal:185`) already folds add+norm+int8-quantize into one dispatch under W4A8 (saves the x_norm DRAM round-trip).

Per layer ×36: **rope 2** (q `:4109`, k `:4119`), **add_inplace 3** (q/k/v bias `:4097/4100/4103`), **memcpy_f32_off 2** (K-append `:4133`, V-append `:4141`). rmsnorm 2 = the already-fused line above. ## Ordered, measurement-gated attempts (cheapest/safest first)

**Attempt 0 (MANDATORY pre-flight, free):** run `DISMANTLE_TCB_TRACE=cpu` and check per-token CPU-encode sum vs Off-mode wall (the ledger's mandated ICB/megakernel pre-flight gate, `dead_levers.md` ICB entry). 0.2 already says GPU-busy ≈76%, CPU encode 0.51%. **If the 324 trivial dispatches cost <1ms/token of CPU encode (they do — that's the whole point of the host-side-overhead kill), then fusing them for *dispatch count* is dead and you stop here.** The only live sub-form is fusing for **DRAM round-trips**, scoped below.

**Attempt A — fold q/k/v bias `add_inplace` into the projection-GEMV epilogue (3→0 dispatches/layer, 108/tok).** Biases are added immediately after the q/k/v GEMVs (`:4096-4104`), in-place on `q_buf`/`k_token_buf`/`v_token_buf`, read by nobody in between. The GEMV kernel's final `y[gid] = shmem[0]` store (pattern at `common.metal:533`) becomes `y[gid] = shmem[0] + bias[gid]`. **Realistic ceiling: the `add_inplace` line is the 2.13% trace entry shared with the residual adds — but those residual adds are ALREADY fused into `add_rmsnorm_fused`, so the 108 `add_inplace` here ARE the bias adds and they are inside the 2.13%.** A 256-row q-bias add over a warm buffer is a ~5µs round-trip; folding 3/layer saves the DRAM re-read of q/k/v (already in cache) + 108 dispatch encodes. **Net ceiling ≈ the bias share of 2.13%, call it ≤1.5% GPU-time, almost all irreducible (the add itself still happens in-register).** Risk: the `_pair` fused k+v GEMV and the predec/W4A8 GEMV variants each need a bias-epilogue twin (≈6 kernel variants × bias-on/off) — large surface, and biases only exist on Qwen2 (the `if let Some(qb)` guards at `:4096`).

**Attempt B — eliminate the two KV-append `memcpy_f32_off` (72/tok, 0.83% GPU) by writing the k/v GEMV output directly into the cache slice.** Today k/v proj writes `k_token_buf`/`v_token_buf` (`:4042-4043`), then two `memcpy_f32_off_tcb` copy into `k_cache_buf`/`v_cache_buf` at `slot_kv_off_elems` (`:4131-4148`). The copy exists because (a) RoPE-on-k runs on `k_token_buf` after the copy-source is produced but the trace shows rope on q only writes q_buf — **wait: rope_q at `:4119` rotates k_token_buf BEFORE the memcpy at `:4133`?** No — read order: bias (`:4100`) → rope q (`:4109`) → rope k (`:4119`) → memcpy K/V (`:4133/4141`). So the copy is pure relocation of already-RoPE'd k. **Fold by passing `(k_cache_buf, slot_kv_off_elems)` as the k-proj GEMV output target directly** (the `_pair` GEMV writes two outputs at given offsets — it can write into the cache slice), then rope-in-place on the cache slice via `rope_slice_f32_inplace` (`common.metal:466`, already exists, takes an offset). **Ceiling: 0.83% GPU-time + 72 dispatch encodes. This is the cleanest genuine DRAM-round-trip kill** (the copy is a real 256-elem×2 write+read eliminated, not just a dispatch). Risk: the `_pair` k+v GEMV output offsets must land in the per-layer cache stride; mha then reads from cache as it already does — verify `layer_kv_off_bytes` (`:4151`) math still indexes the same slot.

**Attempt C — fold rope into the q/k projection GEMV epilogue (72/tok, 1.01% GPU).** rope_q (`:4109`) rotates `q_buf` in place right after q_proj; rope_k after k_proj. The GEMV writes `y[gid]`; rope rotates pairs `(y[2p], y[2p+1])` with a per-position cos/sin. **Folding requires the GEMV epilogue to compute cos/sin per output pair** — doable but the GEMV is one-thread-per-row (`common.metal:518`), and rope pairs span two rows, so the epilogue would need cross-row coordination or a second pass within the kernel. **This is the most invasive and least worth it (1.01%, mostly compute that still happens). Scope LAST, likely skip.**

## EXACT parity gate
**Bit-identical** (fusion is a reorder, not a precision change — the charge and plan 2.2 both mandate this):
- (a) `cargo test --release -p dismantle-core --test integration_greedy_64` — greedy-64 SHA vs `tests/golden/_phase0_token_baseline_64.hashes` (the always-on golden, verified present, 32 bytes). MUST be unchanged.
- (b) `dismantle batch-hash --tokens 64` vs a **FRESH HEAD baseline captured before the change** (per scout_3.1's gate (b); historical hashes encode older lever states) → byte-identical (b3sum).
- (c) `phase1_kernel_parity` + any touched-family TCB parity suite green.
- ⚠️ **Bias/rope folding is NOT trivially bit-identical:** `shmem[0] + bias[gid]` vs a separate add over a re-read f32 buffer can differ by FMA-recontraction (~1 ULP), exactly the A10/A5-b trap (`dead_levers.md` A10: −71.67029 vs −71.67032 failed the bit-identical gate). **If the epilogue fold drifts 1 ULP, the bit-identical gate FAILS and the lever is dead-by-gate** — there is no atol escape here because plan 2.2 says hashes must hold. Attempt B (pure memcpy elimination, no arithmetic change) is the only one that is *trivially* bit-identical.

## Ship gate
Paired `tools/bench/paired_lever.sh` delta, B(fused)-vs-A(default). Per 0.1's calibration: **|B/A−1| ≲ 3% is INCONCLUSIVE** (second-position bias + ±5% scatter); a SHIP needs a paired gain clearly beyond 3%, or swap-A/B-and-average. **The honest ceiling of the entire trivial-op set is ~9% GPU-time, and most of that 9% is irreducible in-register compute (the rope rotation, the bias add, the norm — they still execute after fusion); only the DRAM round-trips and encode latency are removed.** Realistic recoverable: Attempt B ~0.8%, Attempt A ~1.5%, Attempt C ~1% — **each is at or below the 3% inconclusive floor.** Stacked best-case maybe ~3-4% GPU-time, which after the 76%-GPU-busy dilution is ~2.5-3% wall = right at the noise floor.

## Kill cross-check (load-bearing — read before scoping)
- **`dead_levers.md` "Host-side per-dispatch overhead (concurrent encoder, PSO batching, gap-closing)" — Type-1 FAMILY kill.** Quote of the resurrection check: *"only if a future kernel restructuring pushes dispatch count per token far higher (CPU encode crossing ~1 ms/token) … The gap itself is closed by making each kernel faster (Stage 2 simdgroup-matrix decode), not by removing dispatch overhead."* Evidence: CPU encode 0.51% of wall; PSO-transition batching 1.06× (free); `gpu_us` accurate at 1.03×. **DISTINCTION: my Attempts A/B/C are NOT scoped as dispatch-COUNT reduction (that's this dead family). They are scoped as DRAM-round-trip elimination — fewer kernels doing the same work with fewer DRAM writes/reads (Attempt B kills a real 256×2-elem copy; Attempt A/B avoid re-reading a buffer the next op would otherwise re-load).** That is the one sub-form the kill explicitly does NOT cover (it covers ICB/concurrent-encoder/PSO/megakernel-for-count). BUT: the round-trips in question are tiny (k/v are 256-elem, biases 256/2048-elem) and the 0.2 trace already prices them at 0.83%+2.13%+1.01% — so even the live sub-form caps below the ship gate. **The fusion is *distinct* from the dead family but lands in the same sub-ship-gate territory for the same root reason: decode is GEMV-bound (86.7%), and everything else is rounding error.**
- **`dead_levers.md` "MoE megakernel" — Type-1.** Resurrection: *"would need a new Apple-Silicon sync primitive that allows cross-TG barriers."* **DISTINCTION: none of my attempts cross a threadgroup barrier.** Attempt A/C are single-kernel epilogues (the GEMV's own final store); Attempt B redirects an output pointer (no new sync). The MoE-megakernel killer (cross-TG sync for gate→down hand-off) does not apply — these are all *within* one kernel's existing TG structure or a pure buffer-target swap. Confirmed clear.
- **`dead_levers.md` "MoE serial route" — Type-1** (one-expert-at-a-time = 50ms vs 44ms): irrelevant, dense path, no expert routing.
- **A5/A6 (`dead_levers.md` decode-kernel micro-opt, Type-1)**: irrelevant to fusion but note the GEMV epilogue edits (Attempt A/C) touch the same kernels A5 would — **do not conflate**; A5 is dead (loads already coalesced), this is an epilogue add, a different edit.

## Strategic caution (blunt)
- **Realistic ceiling: ~2.5-3% wall stacked, i.e. AT the noise floor / inconclusive band.** This is **not** a clean tps win. The 0.2 data is unambiguous: 86.7% of GPU time is the two GEMVs at 52% peak; the entire trivial-op surface is ~9% GPU-time and most of *that* is irreducible compute. The llama 1.55-1.63× gap lives in the GEMV (scout 2.1), not here.
- **Biggest risk: the bit-identical gate kills Attempt A/C outright** (1-ULP FMA drift on the folded epilogue, the A10 precedent), turning a marginal tps probe into a parity failure. **Attempt B is the only trivially-bit-identical, genuinely-GPU-side fusion** — it's the one worth a cheap spike.
- **What kind of win: at best a tiny tps + tiny energy win** (energy tracks GPU-time per 0.3, so −0.8% GPU-time ≈ −0.8% J/tok). Not a long-context win, not a portability win.
- **Recommendation to orchestrator: do Attempt 0 (the `DISMANTLE_TCB_TRACE=cpu` pre-flight) and Attempt B (KV-append memcpy elimination) ONLY — it's cheap, bit-identical, and the cleanest real round-trip kill. Treat A as a second probe gated on B showing signal. Skip C. If Attempt B's paired delta is inside ±3% (likely), record Phase 2.2 as effectively dead-for-tps (NOT a new Type-1 — it's covered by the host-side-overhead family + the GEMV-bound reality; the round-trip sub-form is simply too small) and route all dispatch-reduction energy back to scout 2.1 (GEMV BW efficiency), which is where the gap actually is.** The cheap offline oracle that would resurrect a bigger fusion: a `DISMANTLE_TCB_TRACE=cpu` run showing dispatch-count CPU-encode crossing ~1ms/token (it won't at 616/tok) — same oracle the ICB kill names.
