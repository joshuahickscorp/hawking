## 🧭 Lessons that change how we work

- **In-sample ≠ held-out (EAGLE).** A draft head scoring 90% on its own
  training corpus scored 33% held-out / 13.6% live. Any acceptance/τ number
  from the training corpus is meaningless for ship decisions — always eval on a
  held-out split (nb02 now enforces this). Applies to every learned component.
- **Validate kernels offline before claiming "compiles."** `xcrun metal -c` on
  the concatenated shader source (common+quant+… → AIR) catches MSL syntax
  errors on the CPU, no GPU needed. "Bench/parity GPU-gated" ≠ "syntax unknown."
- **TCB hides per-kernel timing from Instruments.** TokenCommandBuffer bundles a
  token's kernels into one command buffer, so a Metal System Trace sees
  per-command-buffer, not per-kernel, intervals. The homemade TCB trace is
  split-CB-distorted (the §1 gate rejects it). Per-kernel busy-time needs the
  `mst_export.sh` → `mst_analyze.py` path on a real `.trace` bundle.
- **Don't add accumulator chains to a bandwidth-bound GEMV.** `_4r` (4-row ILP)
  was ~−1% vs `_2r`: 2 chains already saturate the bus. Stage-2 levers must cut
  wasted bandwidth/instructions (vectorized unpack, coalesced layout, f16
  scales), not add register pressure.
